Category Overview

Nuclear Gene Expression Control

Epigenetics and gene regulation represent the dynamic mechanisms by which genetic information is interpreted and controlled within the nuclear environment. This collection of 12 comprehensive reviews explores histone modifications, epigenetic inheritance, transcriptional machinery, and the nuclear organization of gene expression programs.

Research Themes

Key areas of investigation in epigenetics and gene regulation

Chromatin States & Modifications

  • Active Chromatin: H3K4me3, H3K27ac, transcriptionally permissive states
  • Repressive Chromatin: H3K27me3, H3K9me3, heterochromatin formation
  • Bivalent Domains: Poised chromatin states in development
  • Pioneer Factors: Transcription factors accessing closed chromatin

Transcriptional Control

  • Enhancer Function: Long-range gene regulation and chromatin loops
  • Promoter Architecture: Core promoter elements and transcription initiation
  • Transcriptional Pausing: RNA polymerase II regulation and elongation
  • Co-transcriptional Processing: Coupling of transcription and RNA processing

Nuclear Organization

  • Transcription Factories: Spatial clustering of active genes
  • Nuclear Domains: Compartmentalization of transcriptional machinery
  • Chromosome Territories: Spatial organization of gene expression
  • Phase Separation: Condensate formation in gene regulation

Clinical Significance

Disease implications and therapeutic applications

Cancer Epigenetics

  • DNA Methylation: CpG island hypermethylation in tumor suppressors
  • Histone Modifications: Dysregulated chromatin states in malignancy
  • Epigenetic Therapy: DNMT and HDAC inhibitors
  • Biomarkers: Epigenetic signatures for diagnosis and prognosis

Developmental Disorders

  • Imprinting Disorders: Disrupted parent-of-origin gene expression
  • Chromatin Remodeling Diseases: Mutations in chromatin regulators
  • Transcriptional Disorders: Defects in gene regulatory networks
  • Epimutations: Non-sequence changes causing disease

Aging & Longevity

  • Epigenetic Clocks: Age-related DNA methylation changes
  • Chromatin Aging: Loss of chromatin organization over time
  • Senescence: Epigenetic regulation of cellular aging
  • Longevity Pathways: Epigenetic control of lifespan
