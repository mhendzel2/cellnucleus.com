Introduction

Nuclear bodies represent discrete, membrane-free compartments within the eukaryotic nucleus that concentrate specific proteins and nucleic acids to facilitate specialized cellular functions. These dynamic structures play crucial roles in gene regulation, RNA processing, and nuclear organization.

Recent advances in super-resolution microscopy and proteomics have revolutionized our understanding of nuclear body composition, dynamics, and functional significance in normal physiology and disease states.

Major Nuclear Body Types

Nucleolus

The nucleolus represents the most prominent nuclear body, serving as the primary site of ribosomal RNA synthesis and ribosome assembly. This tri-partite structure consists of fibrillar centers, dense fibrillar components, and granular components.

Nuclear Speckles

Nuclear speckles, also known as SC35 domains, are enriched in pre-mRNA splicing factors and play essential roles in RNA processing and gene expression regulation.
